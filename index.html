Your Journey to Motherhood

A comprehensive guide to prenatal health, helping you nurture yourself and your growing baby through every stage of pregnancy.

Start Your Journey

Essential Steps for New Moms

Follow these important steps to ensure a healthy pregnancy journey for you and your baby.

1

Confirm Your Pregnancy

Take a home pregnancy test and schedule an appointment with your healthcare provider to confirm and begin prenatal care.

2

Choose Your Healthcare Provider

Select an OB-GYN, midwife, or family doctor who aligns with your birth preferences and makes you feel comfortable.

3

Start Prenatal Vitamins

Begin taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid, iron, and DHA to support your baby's neural development and your health.

4

Schedule Regular Checkups

Attend all prenatal appointments. First trimester: monthly. Second trimester: every 2-3 weeks. Third trimester: weekly.

5

Eat a Balanced Diet

Focus on whole foods: fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and dairy. Avoid raw fish, unpasteurized foods, and excess caffeine.

6

Stay Active Safely

Engage in moderate exercise like walking, swimming, or prenatal yoga. Aim for 30 minutes most days, with your doctor's approval.

Baby's Development Journey

Watch your little one grow through each trimester with these developmental milestones.

First Trimester

Weeks 1-12 | Size: Poppy seed to lime

  • Heart begins to beat (week 5-6)
  • Brain and spinal cord form
  • Tiny arms and legs appear
  • Facial features develop
  • All major organs begin forming
  • Fingers and toes separate

Second Trimester

Weeks 13-27 | Size: Lemon to cauliflower

  • You may feel first movements (quickening)
  • Gender can be determined
  • Hearing develops - baby hears your voice
  • Eyebrows and eyelashes grow
  • Fingerprints form
  • Regular sleep/wake cycles begin

Third Trimester

Weeks 28-40 | Size: Eggplant to watermelon

  • Lungs mature for breathing
  • Brain develops rapidly
  • Baby gains weight quickly
  • Bones harden (skull stays soft)
  • Baby moves into birth position
  • Full term at 39-40 weeks

Safe Prenatal Exercises

Stay active with these pregnancy-safe workouts to boost energy and prepare for birth.

Walking

A low-impact exercise perfect for all trimesters. Aim for 20-30 minutes daily at a comfortable pace.

Swimming

Supports your growing belly while providing a full-body workout. Great for reducing swelling and back pain.

Prenatal Yoga

Improves flexibility, reduces stress, and teaches breathing techniques useful during labor.

When to Call Your Doctor

Know these warning signs that require immediate medical attention.

Heavy Bleeding

Any significant vaginal bleeding, especially with pain or cramping

Severe Headache

Persistent headache with vision changes or swelling (signs of preeclampsia)

Decreased Movement

Noticeable decrease in baby's movements after 28 weeks

Fluid Leaking

Gush or trickle of fluid from vagina (possible water breaking)

Severe Abdominal Pain

Intense pain that doesn't subside, with or without bleeding

Signs of Infection

Fever over 100.4°F, painful urination, or unusual discharge
